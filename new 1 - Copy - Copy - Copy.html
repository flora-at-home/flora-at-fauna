Try It Yourself

What I did

I wanted to apply the knowledge I gained about Hydroponics by growing my own kitchen garden. Here’s what I did. I gathered some recyclable material from my garage and started to set up my own simple Hydroponics model using a few seeds from the kitchen.


    Sowing and Setting Up

  1. I placed a sieve on top of a plastic box (which had been filled with water till the sieve’s bottom).
  2. I lined the sieve with a layer of tissue paper on which I placed my seeds. I covered the seeds with another layer of tissue paper and sprinkled some water on it.

  3. I allowed the seeds to germinate by keeping them in a dark place. The germination took 2-3 days. The germinated seeds had developed roots which grew past the sieve into the water.

  4. Now, I arranged my grow tray. I had made cavities at the base of a grow tray to allow the roots to grow freely.

  5. I fixed Jute wicks to the base of a few small perforated plastic cups and later filled the cups with Coco-peat.


    6. Germinating and Growing

  6. I carefully removed my seedlings from the sieve without damaging the roots and planted them individually in the perforated plastic cups.

  7. I planted these plastic cups in the cavities on the growing tray so that all the seedlings were spaced out evenly. The wicks from the perforated plastic cups were dangling from the bottom.

  8. Now I set up the growing tray onto the tub in which I had taken water. The wicks were set to a length that would allow them to draw water from the reservoir(tub) to the plants in the growing tray.

  9. I kept this setup in the shade in my garden so that the direct sunlight doesn’t harm the slender stalks and allowed them to grow.

  10. To the water in the tub, I added a solution of 30-30-30 Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium. These were the nutrients my plants required. I add these nutrients on a weekly basis
